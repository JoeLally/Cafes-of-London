Cafes Of London

Here, we will look at some cafes in London that are very different. They are in very different parts of London, so you have no reason not to visit!

The Attendent

London is a place brimming with history and this first cafe is no different.This coffee shop may not be to everyone's taste as it was built in 1890 as a Victorican toilet. It offers espresso and breakfast, as well as lunch and brunch.

How do I get there?

This small cafe is located outside the crown and septure pub on Foley Street. It is an 8 minute walk from Oxford Circus tube station. When you exit the station head north, turning right on Foley street.

Sugar Pot

We are a local family, (three siblings) born and raised in the area for over 30 years. Our vision was to create a vibrant, homely, family run coffee shop that aids in building a collective culture that supports the communities we serve through quality, ethics and sustainability.

How do I get there?

Located off Kennington Park road, the closest tube station is Oval. It is along the bus route of the 155 and 333.
